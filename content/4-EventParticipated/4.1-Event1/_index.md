# Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day”

### Event Objectives

- **Long Hoang** shared how to **level up** in your career and day-to-day work
- **Thinh Nguyen** shared about **AI hallucination** — what it is, why it happens, and how to work with AI more safely
- **Khang Nguyen** shared **survival skills** for job hunting and thriving in an office environment
- **Thao Nguyen Phuong** shared how to **vibecode** using the **BMAD** method

### Speakers for making this happen

- **Khang Nguyen** – Solution Architect at CloudKinetics
- **Thinh Nguyen** – DevOps Engineer | FCAJ Ambassador at AWS
- **Long Hoang** – FCAJ Ambassador at AWS
- **Thao Nguyen Phuong** – Cloud Engineer Intern at VIB | FCJ Ambassador at AWS

### Key Highlights

#### Long Hoang – Leveling up at work

- Mindset and habits for continuous growth in a tech career  
- Practical ways to improve skills, visibility, and impact in the workplace  
- Lessons from an FCAJ Ambassador on staying motivated and progressing step by step  

#### Thinh Nguyen – AI hallucination

- What **AI hallucination** means when models generate plausible but incorrect information  
- Common causes and risks when relying on AI in DevOps and engineering workflows  
- Tips to verify outputs, prompt more effectively, and use AI as a helper—not a single source of truth  

#### Khang Nguyen – Job hunting and office survival skills

- How to prepare for interviews and present yourself clearly to employers  
- Soft skills and professional habits that help you succeed after joining a company  
- Real-world advice from a Solution Architect on adapting to team culture and office expectations  

#### Thao Nguyen Phuong – Vibecoding with BMAD

- Introduction to **vibecoding** — a flow-focused way of building with AI-assisted development  
- Overview of the **BMAD** method and how its phases structure planning, building, and iteration  
- Reference: [BMAD Method documentation](https://docs.bmad-method.org)  

### Key Takeaways

- Career growth is intentional: set goals, learn consistently, and seek feedback like **Long Hoang** emphasized  
- When using AI tools, watch for **hallucination** and always cross-check critical facts and commands (**Thinh Nguyen**)    
- Job search and onboarding go smoother with preparation, communication, and professionalism (**Khang Nguyen**)  
- Structured methods like **BMAD** can make AI-assisted coding more productive and less chaotic (**Thao Nguyen Phuong**)  

### Applying to Work

- Build a personal **level-up plan** (skills, certifications, side projects) and review it each month  
- Add a **verification step** whenever AI generates code, configs, or documentation before you commit or deploy  
- Refine CV, portfolio, and interview stories using **job-hunting** tips from the session  
- Try **BMAD** on a small personal or internship task and document what worked in your workflow  

### Event Experience

Attending **“Community Day FCAJ HCM”** was extremely valuable, connecting me with the FCAJ community in Ho Chi Minh City and learning from practitioners across cloud, DevOps, and AWS programs. Key experiences included:

#### Learning from the organizing team
- **Khang Nguyen**, **Thinh Nguyen**, **Long Hoang**, and **Thao Nguyen Phuong** helped make the event happen and shared perspectives from solution architecture, DevOps, and cloud engineering.  
- Sessions and discussions reinforced practical skills and community practices relevant to FCAJ and FCJ members.  

#### Session highlights
- **Long Hoang** inspired a clearer view of how to grow at work instead of waiting passively for opportunities.  
- **Thinh Nguyen** helped me understand **AI hallucination** and why verification matters in DevOps and daily coding.  
- **Khang Nguyen** gave practical guidance on **interviews, office culture**, and habits that help new graduates fit in faster.  
- **Thao Nguyen Phuong** introduced **vibecoding with BMAD**, opening a structured path to explore [docs.bmad-method.org](https://docs.bmad-method.org).  

#### Networking and discussions
- Met FCAJ/FCJ members, exchanged experiences about internships, AWS learning paths, and community activities.  
- Open Q&A after each talk made it easy to ask follow-up questions about career and AI usage.  

#### Lessons learned
- **Leveling up** is a long-term process: small, consistent improvements beat one-off effort.  
- Use AI with **critical thinking**; hallucinations are a real risk for configs, commands, and explanations.  
- **Soft skills** matter as much as technical skills when applying for jobs and working in a team.  
- A method like **BMAD** can turn “vibecoding” from random prompting into a more repeatable workflow.
